What We Do?

The SLCB runs successful Sri Lanka teams, oversees domestic and recreational cricket, and runs participation programmes that get children active and playing cricket. Through cricket we want to improve lives and connect communities.

Sri Lanka Cricket

The SLCB runs a number of Sri Lanka teams that compete on a global stage:

  • Men's
  • Women's
  • Physical Disability
  • Learning Disability
  • Deaf
  • Blind

Our Sri Lankan teams have the ability to engage and inspire on and off the field. We invest in developing and creating winning Sri Lankan teams and play a range of formats – Tests, 50-over One Day Internationals and 20-over T20s. Grassroots and the elite game go hand-in-hand; every domestic and Sri Lankan player begins their journey on a local pitch.

Sri Lankan Men and Sri Lankan Women – led by Dasun Shanaka and Chamari Athapaththu respectively, men's team are past ODI world champions. The Sri Lankan Men’s Test team are captained by Dimuth Karunarathne.

Sri Lanka Cricket U19

The Sri Lankan Under-19 cricket team is the cricket team that represents Sri Lanka in International Under-19 Cricket. It consists of school-aged cricketers

Sri Lankan U19 team plays a range of formats – Tests, 50-over One Day Internationals and 20-over T20s. Captain of the U19 team is Nipun Dhananjaya, team coach is Hashan Thilakaratne and team manager is Farveez Maharoof.

Domestic Cricket

The Lanka Premier League is a professional cricket league in Sri Lanka in Twenty20 format contested every year by teams representing Sri Lankan cities from 2020. The league was founded by Sri Lanka Cricket in 2018. 5 teams are playing for LPL and they are:

  • Colombo Kings
  • Galle Gladiators
  • Jaffna Stallions
  • Kandy Tuskers
  • Dambulla Viiking

The first edition was hold on 2020 and next edition is going to held on 2021. The current champions are the Jaffna Stallions, who won the inaugural season.

Who We Are?

Sri Lanka Cricket (SLC) is the governing body for cricket in Sri Lanka. It was first registered with the Sri Lankan Ministry of Sports as the Board of Cricket for Sri Lanka (BCCSL) on 30 June 1975 as a national sports body. The board was renamed in 2003. The SLC operates all of the Sri Lankan national representative cricket sides, including the Men's, Women's and Under-19 sides. The SLC is also responsible for organising and hosting Test tours and one day internationals with other nations, and scheduling the home international fixtures.

Our Values

  • Intergrity

    • We strive to protect Sri Lankan Cricket from activities that are detrimental to its integrity by means of disciplinary protocols that ensure fair-play.

  • Transparency

    • We aim to achieve transparency through our sound governance protocols.

  • Professionalism

    • We believe in portraying professionalism in our conduct, behaviour and attitude as the chief body of national cricket in Sri Lanka.

  • Team Work

    • We are a diverse team who possess our own unique ideas. Through the collaboration of our individual uniqueness we achieve collective success.

  • Accountability

    • We as a team believe in accountability and taking responsibility for our actions.

  • Innovation & Creativity

    • Innovation and Creativity are co-dependent to thrive in a competitive game such as cricket. This helps us to tackle unexpected situations in a competent manner.

Our Vision & Mission

  • To be the best cricketing nation with the signature benchmark in consistency, stability andsustainability in all departments of the game.
  • Sri Lanka Cricket will be the most sought after resource center in the world that would shape and fashion the future of the game of cricket all over the world.
